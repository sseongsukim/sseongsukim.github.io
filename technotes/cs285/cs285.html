CS285: Deep Reinforcement Learning

Notes from UC Berkeley's Deep Reinforcement Learning lecture by Professor Sergey Levine.

These notes are based on materials from CS 285, 2023 and CS 185/285.

01 Imitation Learning Analyze the Distributional Shift Problem and Implement Behavior Cloning Algorithms. 02 Policy Gradient Direct policy optimization through likelihood-ratio gradients, variance reduction, and natural policy gradients. 03 Actor-critic & Value-based RL Value functions, Q-learning, actor-critic methods, advantage estimation, and bootstrapping. 04 Control as Variational Inference A probabilistic view of control, maximum entropy RL, and inference-based policy optimization. 05 RL with LLMs Reinforcement learning for language models, reward modeling, preference optimization, and alignment. 06 Model-based RL Learning dynamics models, planning with rollouts, model predictive control, and compounding model error. 07 Offline RL Learning from fixed datasets under distribution shift, conservative objectives, and behavior constraints. 08 RL Theory Foundational guarantees, Bellman operators, policy improvement, sample complexity, and regret. 09 Exploration Exploration strategies, intrinsic motivation, optimism, uncertainty, and information gain.
